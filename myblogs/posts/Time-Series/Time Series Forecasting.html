Time Series Components

  1. Trend: General direction in which something is developing or changing. Pattern exhists when there is a long-term increase or dec in data.

  2. Seasonality: A clear pattern repeating at a regular time interval. For E.g., Spike in summer, and dips in winter for caprie or shorts sales. (quarterly m=4, monthly m=12, dayOfTheWeek=7)

  3. Cyclic Trend: Cyclic pattern but donâ€™t have a constant frequency. Looks like seasonal but it not seasonal. They occur across longer period of times. No fixed period. Average length of cycle is longer than seasonal pattern. E.g., Ecomomic increase or recession in a country over a period of time. If you can predict the peaks and troughs in general, it is a seasonal trend otherwise it is a cyclic trend.

  4. Irregularities, missing data: A point of anamoly. This is separate domain called as Anamoly detection.

TS Decomposition

Time Series exhibit variety of patterns. So it is useful to split TS into several components, each representing a pattern category.

  1. Trend-cycle component (\(T_{t}\))
  2. Seasonal component (\(S_{t}\))
  3. Residual component (\(R_{t}\))

Can be additive or multiplicative. 1. Additive - Does not vary with the average value of the series, the level 2. Multiplicative - Appears to be proportional to the level of time series
